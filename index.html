Some things I'm passionate about:

Currently, I'm the Senior Data Science & Engineering Lead at DataCulture, working to build infrastructure and inform strategy to create data-driven organizations.

I just came off a busy campaign season, starting off as a Data Scientist on Elizabeth Warren's campaign in July 2019.


Later on I joined the Biden campaign as a Senior Analytics Engineer focusing on early vote data, and stayed on for the Georgia runoff senate race.

I came into the campaign world straight off of a Masters in Computational Analysis and Public Policy from UChicago, and got my undergraduate degree at NYU.

Through it all, I like to stay connected to the broader community.

After the Warren campaign, I gave a career talk at the Shishu Bharati of Nashua, focusing on the roles Indian-Americans can and do play in the world of politics.
Last summer, I contributed to the Parson's wrapper for the Rock the Vote API to include all the ways we were using their data to track voter registrations.
More recently, I spoke at the DataForProgress data engineering open mic
about the early vote and ballot cure engineering pipelines used in the general and runoff election, and about leveraging Twitter data on campaigns.
