Natasha Mathur

Some things I'm passionate about:
tackling thorny data problems, progressive politics, finding my next favorite book

Currently, I'm a Principal (Data Science) at Precison Strategies, working with political and non-political organizations
to get the most out of the data and the technology they have access to.

I got my start in politics as a Data Scientist on Elizabeth Warren's presidential campaign, which I joined in July 2019.

Later on, I joined the Biden campaign as a Senior Analytics Engineer focusing on early vote data,
and stayed on to lead early vote data engineering for the Georgia runoff Senate races.

I entered the campaign world straight off of a Masters in Computational Analysis and Public Policy from UChicago, and got my undergraduate degree at NYU.

Through it all, I like to stay connected to the broader community.

After the Warren campaign, I gave a career talk at the Shishu Bharati of Nashua, focusing on the roles Indian-Americans can and do play in the world of politics.
In the summer of 2020, I contributed to the Parson's wrapper for the Rock the Vote API to include all the ways we were using their data to track voter registrations.
This past year I've spoken at the DataForProgress data engineering open mic about the early vote and ballot cure engineering pipelines used in the general and runoff election,
and about leveraging Twitter data on campaigns.
More recently, I've been a mentor with Kode with Klossy, and volunteer as an SAT tutor at a local high school.
