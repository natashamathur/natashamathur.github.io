Natasha Mathur

Some things I'm passionate about:
tackling thorny data problems, public health, progressive politics, finding my next favorite book

Currently, I'm the data director at Maisha Meds, helping improve access to healthcare in several African countries.

I started off my career in politics as a Data Scientist on Elizabeth Warren's presidential campaign, which I joined in July 2019.

Later on, I joined the Biden campaign as a Senior Analytics Engineer focusing on early vote data,
and stayed on to lead early vote data engineering for the Georgia runoff Senate races.

Afterwards, I worked at Precison Strategies, helping political and non-profit organizations get the most out of the their data and technology.

I entered the campaign world straight off of a Masters in Computational Analysis and Public Policy from UChicago, and got my undergraduate degree at NYU.

Through it all, I like to stay connected to the broader community.

After the 2020 election, I presented at the DataForProgress data engineering open mic about the early vote and ballot cure engineering pipelines used in the general and runoff election,
and about leveraging Twitter data on campaigns.

More recently I've spoken on alumni panels at UChicago and NYU, including for a club that was very influential for me in college, USWIB. I also spoke about the role startups play in the public health space at the 2024 Africa Health Conference at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health
