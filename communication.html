Best Practices for Team Communication


Key principles:

1. Nothing should exist only in someone's head

2. Things only get done if it they have a deadline and are assigned to someone.

3. Meetings without an agenda - either internally or client facing - should probably be illegal.

The best system is the one that you and your team will use.

This could be Notion status boards, a running Google doc, Slack, carrier pigeons . . . you do you.




Tips and Tricks

  • Just as no meeting should exist without an agenda, no meeting should end without concrete takeaways and a plan to accomplish them.
    Take a few minutes after each meeting to send out a quick recap, provide a list of requests to the client, and record new items for the team to do.

  • If you have a list of requests, follow up on which ones have and haven't been completed at least once between when you ask and when you need to have them all fulfilled by.

  • Keep in touch with people. Say thanks, emoji react, let them know what the next steps are - make sure everyone is kept in the loop and feels that way. Everyone should feel and be heard.
    Encourage and support actions that you want to promote.

  • Make things as easy and intuitive as possible.
    Remove all possible barriers to entry when it comes to using selected tools, documents, and methods. The clearer and more integrated a next step is, the more likely it is to actually be followed.
